Enigma + Bombe

Enigma

Pressing a letter key sends one electrical signal through the machine. The plugboard may swap the letter, the rotors change it, the reflector sends the same signal back through the rotors, and one output bulb lights up. The lit bulb is the encrypted letter.

Bombe

A Bombe starts with a crib: a guessed word or phrase from the original message. It tries every place the crib could fit under the ciphertext, then searches for Enigma settings whose plugboard consequences do not contradict each other.
